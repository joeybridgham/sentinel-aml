How Sentinel works

A rules-based, explainable monitoring program

Sentinel reviews account activity against a defined set of detection rules drawn from recognized money laundering typologies. Every alert is traceable to the specific transactions that triggered it and the rule that fired, so an analyst can understand, document, and defend each decision. This mirrors how a bank BSA/AML program operates in practice, where examiners expect detection logic to be transparent rather than a black box.

Findings are consolidated by customer. When an account trips more than one rule, the highest scoring finding becomes the headline typology and the others are recorded as additional indicators on the same alert, which keeps the queue readable and reflects how investigators work a relationship rather than isolated transactions.

Detection rules

R1

Structuring

Repeated cash deposits kept below the reporting threshold to avoid a Currency Transaction Report, often split across days or branches.

Logic: 3 or more cash deposits under $10,000 within a 10 day window, or 3 or more in the $8,000 to $9,999 band.
R2

Threshold avoidance

Cash or wire activity clustered just beneath the reporting line, extending the structuring concept to non cash channels.

Logic: 3 or more cash or wire transactions valued $9,000 to $9,999 within a 21 day window.
R3

Round-dollar activity

A concentration of exact round value transfers, which can indicate movement unrelated to genuine commercial invoicing.

Logic: 4 or more wires or transfers in exact multiples of $1,000 valued $5,000 or more.
R4

Rapid pass-through

Funds received and quickly forwarded onward with little residual balance, a hallmark of funnel accounts used for layering.

Logic: an incoming transfer above the customer baseline, with 80% or more disbursed within 48 hours to 3 or more counterparties.
R5

Dormant reactivation

A long quiet account that suddenly moves significant value, a known indicator of account takeover or misuse.

Logic: inactivity of 40 or more days followed by $15,000 or more in activity within two weeks.
R6

Velocity spike

A sharp deviation from the customer's established activity profile that warrants review even without another typology present.

Logic: weekly volume exceeding 4 times the expected weekly baseline and $40,000.
R7

High-risk geography

Cross border activity with jurisdictions subject to FATF action, which calls for enhanced due diligence.

Logic: any counterparty in a FATF call for action or increased monitoring jurisdiction; severity scales with value and PEP status.
R8

Sanctions match

Counterparty names screened against the sanctions list using fuzzy matching to catch transliterations and aliases.

Logic: name similarity to an OFAC SDN entry of 85% or higher is a confirmed match; 55 to 84% is a potential match for review.

Customer risk rating

Each customer carries a risk rating that informs alert prioritization. Ratings reflect known KYC factors including customer type, expected versus observed activity, geographic exposure, and politically exposed person status. Higher rated customers receive closer scrutiny and their alerts score higher.

Alert lifecycle

01

Detection

Rules run across the transaction set and emit findings, consolidated into one alert per customer.

02

Triage

The analyst reviews the queue by severity and clears clear false positives with a documented reason.

03

Investigation

Genuine alerts are escalated to a case and the underlying activity and counterparties are examined.

04

Disposition

The case is resolved as unsubstantiated or advanced for reporting, with the rationale recorded.

05

Reporting

Where warranted, a Suspicious Activity Report narrative is drafted and filed with FinCEN.

On false positives. Most alerts in any real monitoring program are not suspicious. The value of an analyst lies in clearing routine activity quickly and accurately while surfacing the cases that matter. Sentinel deliberately includes benign activity that trips a rule, such as a legitimate cash business or a one off cross border supplier payment, so the false positive workflow is part of the demonstration rather than an afterthought. Production systems typically see far higher alert volumes and clear rates than this curated sample.
